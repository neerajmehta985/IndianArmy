About Indian Army

The world's second-largest standing army with a rich history of valor

Our History

The Indian Army was formed in 1895 under the British Raj. After India's independence in 1947, it became the national army of the Dominion of India, and later the Republic of India. The Indian Army has fought in four wars with Pakistan, one with China, and has participated in numerous peacekeeping missions.

Our Mission

The primary mission of the Indian Army is to ensure national security and national unity, to defend the nation from external aggression and internal threats, and to maintain peace and security within its borders.

Organization

The Indian Army is organized into seven commands, each headed by a Lieutenant General. It has a strength of over 1.4 million active personnel, making it the world's second-largest standing army.

Gallantry Awards

Param Vir Chakra

India's highest military decoration awarded for the highest degree of valor in the presence of the enemy.

Maha Vir Chakra

The second highest military decoration for acts of conspicuous gallantry in the presence of the enemy.

Vir Chakra

Awarded for acts of gallantry in the presence of the enemy on the battlefield.
